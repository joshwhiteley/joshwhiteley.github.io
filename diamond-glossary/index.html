metrics

potency

AUCn: normalized area under the dose-response curve up to the n % growth inhibitory concentration [ICn]

Einf: effect at ∞ drug concentration (maximum achievable effect)

GRinf*: normalized growth inhibition effect at ∞ drug concentration (maximum achievable effect) [partially deprecate]

GRmax: maximum growth inhibition effect at highest drug concentration (measured maximum effect)

interactions

Bliss independence model: The Bliss independence model assumes a stochastic process in which two drugs elicit their effects independently, and the expected combination effect can be calculated based on the probability of independent events [reference]

Loewe additivity model: The Loewe additivity model calculates the expected response as if both drugs are the same. Hence, a slice through the response surface at any chosen effect should show a linear relationship between the doses of the two drugs [reference]

FICn: fractional inhibitory concentration at n% growth inhibition.

FBCn: fractional bactericidal concentration at n% growth inhibition.

Bliss FICn: Bliss synergy score for a combination at [ICn]

Loewe FICn: Loewe additivity score for a combination at [ICn]

other

Hill: The estimated Hill slope of a dose response (DR) curve.

models

computational modeling overview

most of the models referred to are Random Forest models. typically, what is changing across the different models that we have are one of multiple things:

- subconditions: typically, not all subconditions measured are included in a model. an example of a subcondition model would be adv, which would contain acidic, dormancy and valerate features for each combination.

- labels: an n label model refers to the amount of labels that are present in the train/test dataset (essentially, the amount of combos we have known information about). the labels themselves (ideally) never change, and are binary variables encoding either better or worse than the reference combo. the DiaMOND team uses 54 and 94 label models currently, 94 being the most updated set, 54 being the set for publication.

- reference combo: there are two main combinations that groups doing mouse studies benchmark against, BPaL, and HRZE. most of the RMM work is relative to BPaL, most of the Kramnik work is relative to HRZE. SoC (standard of care) refers to HRZE.

- input data: often times, the experimental team will generate data via experiments to fill in "data holes", essentially increasing our coverage of measurements against drug pairs.

in vivo models

relapsing mouse model (RMM): most of the work the DiaMOND team does is using labels from combinations that have been tested in the relapsing mouse model (typically Balb/c). the primary outcome we look for in these studies are better or worse than the reference combo. [reference]

kramnik model: more recently, groups have been performing combo performance tests in the kramnik mouse due to its ability to create lesions more true to the human environment. the most common outcome here is better or worse than the reference combo, but there are other data points that are measured on a per experiment basis. [reference]

marmoset: we've been given a dataset that for a combination study on marmosets done through the NIH. typically, these are PET/CT measurements for individual lesions. [reference]

human: we've been given a couple of different human datasets for small numbers of combinations through the NIH. notable, one is an EBA study, through NexGEN EBA, but we have recently been given access to a much broader human dataset. typically these are PET/CT measurements for individual lesions. [reference]

modeling terms

model performance

AUC (area under the curve): a metric that measures a model's performance by quantifying its ability to distinguish between positive and negative classes [higher is better]

F1 score: the harmonic mean of a model's precision and recall [higher is better]

AUCF1: a simple addition of the AUC score and F1 score of a model [higher is better]

P(>BPaL): the probability (via a classifier) of a specific combination being better than BPaL [higher is better*]

P(>SoC): the probability (via a classifier) of a specific combination being better than the Standard of Care, HRZE [higher is better]

confidence intervals: statistically calculated confidence intervals of predictions done at some α%, typically via infinitesimal jackknifing

(infinitesimal) jackknifing: technique to measure variability of predictions from a random forest classifier [reference]
